# Public PDF Report Storyboard

Target length: 12-16 pages.

Audience: public stakeholders, state and local planners, elected officials,
agency staff, media, and interested residents.

Use the 2024 SDC PDF as the pacing model: a compact public report with a clear
statewide story, a short methodology section, regional and county context, and
a county appendix. The 2026 report should keep that approachable structure
while staying baseline-only in the public package.

## Story Arc

The report should answer five reader questions:

1. What do the 2026 baseline projections say about North Dakota's statewide population?
2. What assumptions shape the public baseline?
3. Where is growth concentrated, and where is decline projected?
4. How does the age structure change?
5. Where can users find exact values after reading the PDF?

The Baseline (CBO-Adjusted) scenario carries the main narrative thread. The
unadjusted trend-continuation path is internal only, and the restricted/high
sensitivities are not part of the public package.

## Page-By-Page Storyboard

| Page | Reader takeaway | Likely content and exhibit |
|------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| 1 | This is the official public projection report for 2025-2055. | Cover, horizon, agency attribution, North Dakota map or clean population graphic. |
| 2 | The report is short enough to navigate quickly. | Table of contents matching the 2024 report style. |
| 3 | Statewide growth in the public baseline is the lead story. | Executive summary with key-number callout: 2025 population and 2055 baseline. |
| 4 | The projections are conditional planning assumptions. | Plain-language baseline description covering the CBO additive migration adjustment and the -5% fertility adjustment. |
| 5 | The statewide path is easier to read as a single baseline line. | 2025-2055 line chart with a short caveat that this is a projection, not a guaranteed future. |
| 6 | Key years make the public baseline easier to compare. | Baseline table for 2025, 2030, 2035, 2040, 2045, 2050, and 2055. |
| 7 | Regional change is uneven. | Eight-region map plus baseline change bars. |
| 8 | County growth is concentrated in a smaller group of counties. | Top-growth and largest-decline county bars, or a county map with a compact ranked table. |
| 9 | Age groups do not move in the same direction. | Trend chart for under 18, 18-64, 65+, and 85+. |
| 10 | The age structure is visibly shifting toward older residents. | Population pyramid: state 2025 vs 2055 baseline (side-by-side, overlaid, or stacked) with a short sex-ratio callout. Race/ethnicity only if final review supports a simple public display. |
| 11 | The method is standard and understandable. | Plain-language cohort-component method box covering base data, fertility, mortality, migration, and the baseline construction. |
| 12 | Readers need cautions and download guidance before using the numbers. | Migration sensitivity, geographic concentration, city/place exclusion, and public Excel/CSV description. |
| 13-16 | County users can find their county without opening the workbook. | Baseline key-year county appendix; exact annual values live in downloads. |

## Exhibits To Prepare

- State population by year, 2025-2055, baseline only.
- Baseline key-year table for 2025, 2030, 2035, 2040, 2045, 2050, and 2055.
- Region map and baseline regional change chart.
- Top-growth and largest-decline county exhibit.
- Age-group trend chart for under 18, 18-64, 65+, and 85+.
- Population pyramid for state 2025 vs 2055 baseline.
- County appendix table with selected baseline years.

If space allows, add a small callout showing the share of statewide growth
concentrated in the largest growth counties. Avoid full annual county tables,
city/place tables, and detailed age-sex tables in the PDF.

## Relationship To The 2024 PDF

Keep:

- Sixteen-page public-report scale.
- Cover, table of contents, executive summary, statewide chart, methodology,
  regional section, demographic section, county appendix, and contact page.
- Plain-language interpretation beside charts and tables.

Update:

- Replace multi-scenario language with baseline-only language.
- Extend the horizon to 2025-2055.
- Make the baseline assumptions explicit, including the CBO additive
  migration adjustment and the -5% fertility adjustment.
- Use current cohort-component method language.
- Add clear public download language for one Excel workbook and one CSV.

Move out of the PDF:

- Detailed age-sex tables.
- Technical package inventories.
- City/place projections.
- Internal QA or production notes.
