- Preferred page grid and maximum page count.

- Chart style guidance for line charts, bar charts, and compact tables.
- Accessibility requirements for color contrast, table readability, and PDF tagging.
- Branding assets, fonts, color palette, contact block, and required agency boilerplate.
- Desired file handoff format for tables and chart data.
- Deadline for final data lock to meet publication timing.

## What Marketing Needs From Us

- Final section outline and page priorities.
- Draft executive summary and short caveat language.
- Final public Excel workbook and public CSV after the CF-001 gate.
- Chart-ready data extracts for statewide, region, county, and age-group exhibits.
- County and region names exactly as they should appear.
- A list of figures that must include all three scenarios.
- QA signoff that final public files exclude city/place projections.

## Placeholder Guidance For Design

Marketing can begin with:

- Page count and section order from `pdf-content-outline.md`.
- Prior PDF structure from `data/raw/ND Population Projections.pdf`.
- Placeholder values from current March 2026 exports for layout only.
- Final wording flags from ADR-042 and this folder.

Marketing should not treat any current numbers as final until the data team provides the final public Excel/CSV package.

## Intake Talking Points

- The report should lead with the baseline trend-continuation story but keep the scenario range visible.
- The public document covers state, regions, and counties only.
- City/place projections are not part of this release.
- The downloadable data should be easy for nontechnical users: one workbook and one CSV.
- The PDF should be readable on its own, with the downloads serving users who need exact values.
